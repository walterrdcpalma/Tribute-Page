Freddie Mercury

The man who saved rock

Here's a time line of Freddie Mercury life

  • September 5, 1946 - Freddie Mercury was born in Zanzibar, Tanzania on September 5, 1946
  • 1954 - At the Age of eight, Freddie began his education at St. Peters English boarding school (near Bombay, India)
  • 1964 - Freddie Mercury moved to Middlesex, England in 1964, at the age of 17.
  • 1967 - In 1967, Freddie Mercury joined a band called Smile.
  • 1970 - In 1970, Freddie Mercury took over as the lead singer of Smile, and renamed the band Queen.
  • 1975 - The album "A Night At The Opera" was released in 1975
  • 1975 - The song Bohemian Rhapsody was released on the album entitled "A Night at the Opera" in 1975
  • 1975 - In 1975, Freddie Mercury toured Japan with Queen
  • 1977 - The song "We Will Rock You" was released on the album entitled "News of the World" in 1977
  • 1978 - The song Bicycle Race was released in 1978 on the album Jazz
  • 1980 - The song "Another One Bites the Dust" was released on the album entitled "The Game" in 1980
  • 1980 - The album "The Game" was released in 1980
  • 1984 - In 1984, Queen decided to take a break. During that break, Freddie Mercury decided to release a solo album called Love Kills.
  • 1988 - The Open Air festival in Barcelona, on October 8th, was Freddie Mercury's last performance.
  • 1991 - The album Innuendo was released in 1991
  • November 23, 1991 - The day before he died, Freddie Mercury announced that he had AIDS.
  • November 24, 1991 - He died of bronchial pneumonia on November 24, 1991
  • 2001 - In 2001, Queen was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in Cleveland, Ohio
  • 2008 - In 2008, Rolling Stone ranked Freddie Mercury 18th on their list of the 100 greatest singers of all time.
